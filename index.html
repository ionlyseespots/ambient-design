Ambient Design *

am·bi·ent /'ambēənt/ adjective

  1. of the surrounding area or environment
  2. completely surrounding; encompassing
  3. creating a certain reaction or mood, often a subconscious one, by being wherever people tend to be
  4. pertaining to or noting sounds that create a peaceful and relaxed atmosphere
  5. pertaining to or noting close and constant social contact and communication fostered by the Internet or the use of digital devices

* View and/or debug ambient design code:

  1. Core design pattern
  2. UX Shells

Ambient design has a core design pattern (landscape & portrait) for the HTML as a presentation format without the need for structure based classes.

Ambient design is methodology for creating layout. There are 5 distinct design patterns:

  1. Typography
  2. UI Elements
  3. UX Shells (first priority)
    panel-shell & multi-column-shell design patterns are the first priority, and make up the living (static) template you see here.
  4. Navs
  5. Form States (second priority)
